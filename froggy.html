Jack Frog

Fitness Tracking App

Project Overview

This project demonstrated a User-Centered-Design (UCD) process in a fast-tracked, 3 week design sprint. The goal was to design an Apple Watch fitness application

Key Stages

  • User Research
  • Synthesized Research Results
  • Brainstorming
  • Prototyping
  • Testing
  • Observations

User Research

To understand user needs, I conducted interviews with fitness tracker users, capturing insights on how they use their devices and what features matter most to them. I wanted to understand users experience with fitness tracking apps, user needs and what would entice a non-user to start tracking.

Problem Statement

Following the research I was able to create the problem statement

"I spoke to Jessie who is a person in her late 20s and is very active and has a fitness tracker. I noticed that Jessie is a casual user of the app, she finds it difficult to remain engaged in daily usage and will go through protracted periods of inactivity. I want to design a solution that will encourage Jessie to use the app more frequently, ideally on a daily basis.

Ideation: Crazy 8s

Using the Crazy 8s method, I quickly sketched various design ideas to brainstorm different layouts and interactions for the app.

Prototype

Introducing Jack Frog

Jack Frog is a fitness and lifestyle tracker. He is your friend and training companion. When you hit your goals he is happy and you can buy him little gifts. You and Jack Frog will be great friends, achieve some great things together.

After refining ideas, I created a mid-fidelity prototype in Figma to bring the concept to life. Jack Frog is very visual and I felt a low-fidelity prototype would not capture the feeling of Jack Frog.

Usability Testing

I conducted usability testing with the prototype, observing user interactions and gathering feedback.

Observations

I conducted usability testing with the prototype, observing user interactions and gathering feedback. Below is a list of observations from the usability tests.

  • Observation 1: Users found the step tracking feature intuitive.
  • Observation 2: The navigation could be simplified for quicker access to key features.
  • Observation 3: Users appreciated the customization options available in settings.
